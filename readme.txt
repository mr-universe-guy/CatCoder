catcoder

code drones to uncover the secret about the facility and survive

She awakens cold, hungry and alone. The last thing she remembers was almost a deam, and the last thing she remembers was being given special pills so she would sleep. Why did she need those? where is her mother now? Why is the facility so, so quiet.

The elf girl picks up her mom's tablet and finds a note. "I am so sorry. I know you'll never read this, but I wanted to say it anyways. I wish I could have brought you with me. I hope it was painless. I hope you had fun. I don't deserve the faith you put in me. I am sorry. I love you."

Well that doesn't make any sense, so she uses the device to send a text message to her mom's implanted device like she'd done a thousand times before. "Good morning Mom, sorry I think I overslept. Where are you? Gonna get breakfast and get to work before I get in trouble" The urge for the toilet hit her hard, and shortly after that she was vomiting profusely into the sink. It must have lasted 20 minutes before the device chimed in response.

"Who is this, how did you get into my tablet?"

"It's me mom, duh. Why are you acting weird? I threw up though. Might tell Jake I need to go to the infirmary before I can start my shift."

There was no response after a few minutes so she brushed her teeth and got dressed. She was just opening the door when the device chimed again.

"This is impossible, who are you, what is happening?"

Why's mom being weird? The elf girl shrugged, her stomach hurt after puking so hard and her head was throbbing. Some water and something sugary will help. That's when she realized the door hadn't opened yet. The button clicked but the door buzzed as though it were locked. She twisted the locking bolt back and forth but nothing happened.

"Mom, why's the door locked?"

The wait was excrutiating. Why was mom taking so long to respond? even when she was busy she could type using her computational implant, at most it would be a minute. 5 minutes passed before the device chimed.

"Honey, I'm sorry. I'm so sorry. I don't know what to do. You are alone."

"Mom quit being weird. You're scaring me."

Another 5 minute wait. Her heartbeat was a drum against the back of her skull. She tasted the bile creeping up her throat again.

"I unlocked the device, you'll need to figure it out youself. I'm sorry."

The device beeped and restarted itself. After another several minute wait the screen flashed as text scrolled rapidly across before returning to the home screen, now with several new app icons and another message from mom.

"I'm sorry, good luck."
